I have said many times everyone in the world should get in-depth one on one talk therapy some time in life. The book in the photo is a Japanese Accordian hand-made-one-of-a-kind artist's book (as I like to call them). It is mixed-media--collage w/painting and drawing--It is 17 inches high, eighteen feet long when unfolded, and stretched it's entire length. I made this book at a time when I was still trying in vain to connect with the woman who gave birth to me. In it I illustrated the poetry of the one who called herself my mother. She was a product of a time that seemed to demand of women that they be mother and wife, and while there were women of this time who required no such thing of themselves, the mother of whom I write, the one who gave birth to me, should never have married her husband, should never have had children. She would have opted out of either convention, had she had the courage. Sadly, she did not. So instead, in some misguided effort to adjust the world to a more manageable size that she could dominate, she found the man she felt she might rule over, and then proceeded to give birth to four ( the boy born first and dead) innocent babies: the three girls survived; each was in uniquely damaged by the woman who gave them birth, and the man who called himself their father. The girls grew up together and then each went her separate way, often the sad result in families where there is abuse and neglect. Tragically, the very ones who would naturally have been the greatest support to one another through lives fraught with difficulty due to shared tragedy, cannot bear to face the others. These others, the siblings who know, who have been forced to bear up under what for any child would be unendurable, serve only to remind in later life. This reminder, for the one who seeks not to remember, not to know, not to see, must be avoided, therefore the greatest reminder--the sisters--become a kind of phobia for the seeker of ignorance. Do I repeat myself? It is because I was not and am not the avoider. I have worked diligently over time and with the help of a wonderful therapist to understand a situation which was for most of my life until I entered into therapy, incomprehensible. You see, I still struggle with the monstrous feeling that this is wrong, and worse than wrong: it is stupid. I do not blame my sisters that there exists no comeraderie between us. I blame the woman who gave birth to us and then did her best to destroy us. I blame the man who biologically aided her, and then enabled her in her endeavor to ignore the duties of a mother, and indeed, to do active harm. Lest you think there is no hope in this terrible dilemma, I am here to tell you there is hope. I can say these things, aided by Candace and then Diana (two of them, over time), therefore I am free to write the book of truth of my own life. Her poetry is not bad, I have no regrets about the book in the photo, the artwork led me down the path to me.
