For another review of the book and art, please see the extraordinarily sensitive words of the poet Debra Grace Khattab, on last pg. of 'Gallery', middle image. By the way the typo above, producing not two, but three sss' in my name was mine, not that of the anonymous writer. Although I rather like the spelling better that way, I would have to say it is my own worst criticsm of the book, that is to say, the typograghical errors, and worse, a few spelling errors. I typed it on an old-fashioned electric typewriter, in the days not too long ago, when I did not yet own a computor. No spell check, something this website lacks, as well. I produced it in the year leading up to the exhibit, alas I was my own copy-editor. It was at first intended as a walk-through catalogue to go alongside the artwork in the exhibition of the same title. The first draft written in pencil, the second in black ink directly on the first, and all final drafts were typed on mylar tracing paper, each was placed over the last, then copied, along with photos of the art (hours spent at Copy Central, they no longer have the slide copier---outmoded technology). The visual effect of the text is pentimento. The Italian Renaissance word denotes 'repent,' removal and addition of paint, conveying the feeling of looking through many layers. Actually, it is the perfect metaphor for how the show and book came into being, i.e. what I repented was all the years of silence, conciousness is multi-layered, as is all of my artwork.

Thanks for the endorsement on the back of my book Insomnia (Awakening), written by Stephanie JT Russell: "Insomnia (Awakening) is a conclave of bruised, sleepless beings dreaming of redemption from an endless nightmare of silence. Garsson's calm yet insistent text gives voice to their frozen cries in the darkness, like a symphony whose movements progress from trauma through release, and into transcendence. Her plainspoken, trance-like narrative is the testament of one woman's courageous hejira through the dark night of despair, toward relieved certainty of daybreak."-----Stephanie is the author of several books, including One Flash Lightening, A Samurai Path For Living, and The Zen of Small Things.
