On the occasion of her mother\'s death (Thursday May 28, 2009)
I came home yesterday to a message from my older sister Jane. Our mother lay comotose, and so my sister felt, as she told me, freedom to tell me how our mother was doing. She had been ill for a number of years and wanted it kept secret from her two other daughters. Ann, who goes by May from the name my Guru gave her long ago, Mayana, (a name to remind us that the world is illusion), is the youngest. I, the middle child, was named Rosemary by my parents, and was given the name Chandra (moon) by my guru, when I was twenty. The eldest told me that our mother had been in terrible health for five years, had been diagnosed with Lung Cancer in September, and was now dying a terrible death from the illness. She had been a smoker for many years, including the times of her pregnancies and throughout most of the childhoods of my sisters and me. A bout of Pleuresy, part of her lung removed, and a stay in intensive care finally stopped her habit, when the three of us were at varying stages of our early lives. My sister told me she had kept our mother's secret (one last secret at the end of a long life of them---she was about to turn 86, on May 30), but felt free to tell, now that our mother was in a coma. I believe Jane felt the need to unburden herself on this one relatively small matter, all things considered, after months and years of caring for her mother alone.

I feel the reason our mother did not want May and me let in on it was three-fold: One, an obsessive need to keep secret many matters of great importance as a way of hoarding for herself, and paranoid ideas about self-protection. Two, a fear that the daughters she abused in the most obvious ways might take revenge at a time when she would have been vulnerable, as a little child is most vulnerable. Three, out of fear that we would refuse to help. She returned at the end of her life to trust of her oldest child, one who had been both favored and leaned on early in her life by a parent, her mother; she leaned on her small daughter for help and support. No parent ever has the right to weigh upon a child in this way. This one, the eldest, had rejected her first in young adulthood, was there for her mother at the end of life, as she had been there for her mother at the beginning of her own life. This burden at the end without even trying to involve the other two, once again was selfish, as this mother had been in all things her entire life. This last selfish act was isolatingly, laboriously cruel to Jane. It is said that we die as we lived, and so it was with Jean Garsson. My sister Jane called later last night (first writing of this was the morning of May 28) to tell me our mother had died.

This piece I wrote about my mother about two years ago:........Serpentine is of a mottled, dual appearance. It is an earthing stone, controlling, creative of imbalance. Toxic to blood and body, it leaves one unsure of longevity. Chrysotile cotains some iron. Her color is olive-green, blackish to yellowish green, brown, yellow, rarely she is white. Greasy mentality, she is not to be trusted; she is waxy, can seem silky, but watch out: actually she is splintery and brittle. Usually thought of as massive, she is crypto crystaline in appearance. She remains to this day unknown. She preferred it that way, may have done two of her daughters this kind of possibly permanent damage, they too have secreted themselves away. the one in the middle has managed through the difficult work of several paths, to extricate herself from that tomb. I believe there is hope for the others. Lamellar and columnar, she grew fiber flexible and tough like asbestos, chrisotile or tremolite---she herself would not burn---but could, and did wreak unsalvageable, insideous damage in others, especially her three daughters; the middle one feels the searing in her lungs to this day. Recalling the skin of a serpent this daughter, the midddle one alludes to the surface patterns in her art, easier than descent into the furnace. (apologies and partial credit to Judy Hall, The Crystal Bible).
