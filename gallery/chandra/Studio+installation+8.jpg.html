Lulu's movie "Displaced Artist" has been replaced with "home is where the art is", called that at Youtube, quotation marks and all, but to be found at the home page of this website under Interview with Chandra. I have just recieved a notice where I am living about a major rent increase: $43 of "Banked increases," 3.3% per year for the last two years I've lived here. During that time I have worked for one third of my rent off (from $650) as resident manager. I was instructed never to work more than six hours per week. So, I have been paying $450 per month to live and work here; the job has only occasionally been very demanding. I have done a good job. My fellow tenants like me. The worst thing about the job has been an unwillingness on the parts of my three employers (a landlord and two upper managers) to be at all communicative with me, or the other tenants. They have been willing to fix most of the plumbing problems, that could damage the building. The place is being sold. Interestingly, while I have recieved this notice that my original rent is being increased, my employment has not been terminated. All of this has hit (me and several of my neighbors) just after Thanksgiving, close to Chanukah and Christmas. Linda Maranzana, the attorney who represented me against my prior landlord, asked me when I first met her this same time of year after I recieved a notice to move, "Why do so many landlords pull these shenanegans on their tenants during the Holiday season?"... I could not answer her question then, and it echos through my mind today. Do people learn nothing from the economic events of the past several months about how to treat one another with respect and compassion? is real estate greed of the kind that wreaks havoc in the lives of innocent people unstoppable? Is there a chance that I will be treated fairly in this situation?

I lost so much when I was forced to move. The story of the struggle can be read on the home page of this website, click on Deeproots show with Pedro Moreno. More story on little film by Lulu Hanna, called Displaced Artist, click on Interview with Chandra. I fought long and hard, at last I had to give up what became a ghost, namely a place to really showcase my work, a truly viable workplace for an artist and art teacher. I am so greatful to Aki Sasaki the Webmaster, because of him my work lives on in this alternative playground, the internet. Long live the freedom of the internet! Because of Lottie Rose, I have a studio, a home for my work. To her I will always be grateful. Nevertheless, what was lost was a lot, and it must be mourned.
