Many have thought of me (much as many think of you) as a vast empty chalkboard upon which to write.
The truth is, I am a movie screen.
so much has been projected upon me, my mind reels.
So now the truth: the feast I have set before the world has yet to be noticed, let alone eaten and digested.
For the time being I choose to gently set the same food before the undeserving little brat of a world without eyes to see, without an ear, without taste, until it gets hungry enough to partake of the bounty.
In spite of myself I love the little brat.
I love the ancient Indian tale of the scorpion and the frog.
Because of all the love that is in me, I continue to exist.
I gave a tiny Emerald earing set in gold traded for a drawing long ago to a friend I love.
Another drawing was traded for the silver dragon ring made by a friend in High school that I still wear.
I took care of a 93 year old Quaker lady.
I took care of a 2 week old infant until he was 2 years old.
At Rockridge Cafe today a mother walked by the window where I sat with my friend, with a baby facing out in a stomach carrier. I smiled and waived. she smiled and reached out her hand to hold and waive her baby's hand at me, as she walked. I smiled at my friend and marveled at the personality of the mother who I knew would raise a happy child.
Once a Red-tailed Hawk settled for seven minutes on the edge of the roof eleven feet from my face out of my window where I live.
Often as I read with the radio on my eye will light on an uncommon word at the exact moment the same word is spoken or sung on the radio.
Sometimes I can be in conscious control of what happens in my dreams.
I left home at 17. At eighteen I traveled and then lived in a foriegn city for longer than a year.
I am a decent enough writer.
I am a lousy speller.
I showed three sculptures at the Oakland Museum Sculpture Court and then had to harangue the curator for two weeks before he would shine a light on the three hidden behind the walled area he had the museum build specially for the three, and here's the kicker: the sculpture court is gloriously bathed in natural light through glass walls.
I often wonder: what's the point of presenting my work to the public, when the curator couldn't care less or worse, blatently hides the work he chose?
I am on hiatus from serious presentations.
I am enjoying silly presentations and serious protestations.
I have never owned a car.
I tell anyone who will listen about the possibilities of saving the world by individual and collective conservation of energy, as I simultaneously rail against Drones, Predators, Spyplanes and other robotic warfare.
