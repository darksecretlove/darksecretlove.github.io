"...If there's one work in this show that sims up this sypathetic-yet-helpless state, it's Chandra Garsson's "Homage to Munch."The Homage, in this case, is to the Norwegian expressionist's best known work, "The Scream,"wherein a woman howls in horror against a backdrop whose grotesquely distorted terrestrial features mirror an inner anguish, which has since become synonymous with the horror inflicted on the German (German, Jewish, European, world -Chandra) populace by the National Socialists. But where Monk's woman is truly crying out in distress, Garsson's is mute. Her mouth is wide open---open, in fact, wider than any mouth can anatomically stretch; yet the expression on the face is blank and plaintive, devoid of the horror suggested by the gaping maw. What this contradiction adds up to is a near-perfect portrait of a heart and mind that are utterly disconnected..."
