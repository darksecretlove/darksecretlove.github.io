A good place to review my friend Mary Anderson Parks's books, two novels: one called, They Called Me Bunny, the other, The Circle Leads Home. Mary and I met at a writer's group, which I only attended once. There I made a friend; the first time we met, we conversed over my book in her hands, Insomnia (Awakening). She looked at it on the spot, and has given me excellent feedback, after more than one reading. Later, when I was recovering from minor foot surgery, and was unable to walk, I read a copy she had sent me of They Called Me Bunny, which I read quickly, in fascination. In the book Parks captures the world of an adopted American Indian Girl (encapsulated in book and conversations are excellent reasoning for that name for the group---mainly, it's the name most Indians prefer). The arc of Bunny's growth through alienated girlhood, secrecy enforced by her adopted parents around her origins, through some serious dangers to marginalized, traumatized girls in our American culture--- that's just about one in three, by the way---,friendship, and some serious sleuthing on the part of the heroine, bring her and us to joy in the telling and reading. The Circle Leads Home, also a portrait of a young Indian woman, is another kind of a story altogether. It is about a woman alienated from her mother and the resevation where she grew up, the widow of an African American man who died under tragic circumstances, has left and then returns to the reservation and new-found love with another, and with her mother. Again trauma is at the heart of the main character's sets of dilemmas. Through characters torn inwardly, searching (both novels contain excellent suspence, in one case connected to the solution to a mystery of birth and ethnicity, in the other the solution to corruption and greed concerning the sale of old growth trees for logging) feeling reverberates between page and reader. Bunny focuses mostly on the story of a single character; Circle on the other hand is a more outwardly complex tale of several main players. Both are ultimately about many types of love. I recommend whole-heartedly the reading of They Called Me Bunny, and The Circle Leads Home, by Mary Anderson Parks.
