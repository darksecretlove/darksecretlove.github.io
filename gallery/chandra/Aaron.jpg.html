Aaron, Are you out there? Gosh I would love to hear from you. You used to sleep on my belly every day for an hour or two. I would lightly, oh so gently stroke the top of your head where your skull had not fully grown together yet. I wheeled you around and around the Berkeley Art Museum, wheelchair and stroller accessable. "Hoeee," was your fourth or fifth word, to betoken the huge horse in the giant painting of the American Revolution on the top floor of that museum back then. Your first word was "Nanra" (Chandra), then maybe "mama", next came "goobie", for "excuse me", you always heard me say it as I would walk you around museums, town, even the galleries of downtown S.F. (a couple of times Stephen Wirtz helped me get you in the stroller up the stairs to his gallery as he was comming in at the same time we were). We met when you were first born, I cared for you beginning when you were barely two weeks old, until you were two years old. We truly bonded. Did I mention your other first word? "Dogie"! He was a Rhodesian Ridgeback, funny, I can't recall his name. One day, as we had on so many days,we walked out to play in your back yard and 'Dogie', for the first time jumped up to play with you, and knocked you down. Sadly, you were pretty banged up, little tyke that you were, not yet ready for that kind of play. I had been bugging your parents for a raise in pay, your lawyer pop used the accident as an excuse to fire me. Kelly the maid helped me move from the little in-law apartment up on Quail Ave., weeks later she told me you were still wandering around the house, searching for me, querying,"Nanra? Nanra?" My heart was broken. I wonder about you to this day...I hope you are doing wonderfully well.
